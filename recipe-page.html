Japanese Soufflé Pancakes

Welcome to the ultimate guide for making Japanese soufflé pancakes! These light, fluffy, and jiggly pancakes are a delightful twist on a breakfast classic. With their airy texture and delicate sweetness, they’re perfect for a special brunch or an indulgent treat. Follow this simple recipe to achieve the perfect soufflé pancakes that are soft, pillowy, and absolutely irresistible!

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs (separated)
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup (30g) all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil or butter
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions

  1. Whisk the egg yolks, milk, and vanilla extract until combined. Sift in the flour and baking powder, then mix until smooth.
  2. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites with a hand mixer until foamy. Gradually add sugar, continuing to beat until stiff peaks form.
  3. Gently fold the meringue into the egg yolk mixture in three additions using a spatula.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan over low heat and lightly grease it with oil or butter. Spoon the batter into tall, round mounds. Add 1 tablespoon of water to the pan and cover with a lid to create steam. Cook for about 4 minutes.
  5. Carefully flip the pancakes using a spatula. Cover and cook for another 3–4 minutes until golden and fully set.
  6. Transfer the pancakes to a plate and dust with powdered sugar.
